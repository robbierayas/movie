CHARACTER PROFILE: JOSH

========================================
BASIC INFORMATION
========================================
Name: Josh
Role: Wildcard, changed survivor
Occupation: Theoretical Physicist (former), trapped in n=0.333 for weeks
Status: Alive but fundamentally changed by cube exposure
Age: Similar to Lea (late 20s-early 30s)

========================================
ARCHETYPE
========================================
Primary: The Wildcard - Unpredictable but helpful, operates on different logic, changed by cube exposure
Secondary: The Cassandra - Warns of dangers others don't see, warnings prove true but often too late

Shifts: Brilliant theoretical physicist (Pre-Cube) → Changed survivor with cryptic wisdom (Act 2-3 early) → Increasingly lucid when discussing science (Act 3 late). Transformation is not linear - shifts between cryptic and lucid based on context. Scientific discussion reconnects him to original self. "Talking about the science... it helps. Like finding a path through the noise."

========================================
PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION
========================================
- Lean, almost gaunt from time in cube
- Haunted, intense eyes
- Disheveled appearance
- Wearing Barry's cardigan sweater throughout the movie (Barry wore business casual with cardigan in Act 1 Scene 5)
- Moves strangely but purposefully - adapted to cube's geometry
- Looks like someone who's seen too much
- Still recognizably human but "off"
- Rough, unused voice

========================================
CHARACTER ARC
========================================
Before Cube (Backstory): Brilliant theoretical physicist, 2nd smartest after Barry. Close colleague/friend of Lea with unspoken romantic feelings. Worked closely with Barry and Lea on dimensional physics. Indifferent to corporate ranks, focused on solving greatest problems in field. Trusted expert - Lea wouldn't have let him work alone without complete trust. Got sucked into n=0.333 two weeks before Act 1 Scene 8.

During Cube Weeks (Backstory): Found Barry who died 2 days later - "stopped moving". Josh kept moving. Survived weeks alone in root-space. Took Barry's notebook and cardigan sweater carrying Barry's memory. Fundamentally changed by prolonged dimensional exposure. Learned survival rules through hard experience: "keep moving", "exits lie", "only through". Built understanding of cube's patterns and danger zones. Present when Barry died, carries guilt and trauma.

Act 2 (Scenes 2-10): Enters disheveled and haunted in Scene 2, provides cryptic warnings "cube remembers/hungers/tests", "exits lie", "only through". Joins team as experienced guide. Paired with Lea - their connection survives his transformation. Provides warnings that prove true but come too late: "exits lie" before Sofia's vacuum death (Scene 7), "This room is wrong" before floor dissolution (Scene 8), "Metal. Too much metal" before Edgar's magnetic death (Scene 9). Reveals infinite cube scope "Cubes within cubes within cubes." Scene 10 - immediately chooses Alex's group without debate "I go where the movement goes. Alex keeps moving. I'll guide them", warns Marcus about division. Views team as naive but worth saving. Cassandra figure whose wisdom penetrates through repetition despite communication barriers.

Act 3 (Scenes 23-25): Scene 23 - Becomes completely lucid discussing science with Lea, reveals Barry's notebook hidden under shirt "I didn't think it would help", explains three nuclei in root-space with diagrams, shares Barry's quantameter data, admits "I was there when he died", realizes lab building at orbital overlap zone "INZO built it there deliberately", small smile "talking about the science... it helps. Like finding a path through the noise" - reveals cryptic nature is partially protective distance from trauma, original Josh still exists beneath changed exterior. Scene 25 - Lucid challenging Marcus's authority "You don't give orders here", lists the dead blaming Marcus "Your team is dead... You led them here and they died", examines memory device "If it's wrong, I'll fix it", sees through Marcus's control tactics "You threaten them, but you need them. You can't get out alone", warns cube still active "Still pulling people in. They never shut it down", final certainty "Only through. Never out". Fate: Presumably escapes with survivors, changed but not lost, original self accessible through scientific engagement.

========================================
DIALOGUE STYLE
========================================
**Cryptic Patterns (Act 2-3 Early - Survival Mode):**
- Cryptic and fragmented with statements that almost make scientific sense but assume "cube experience" knowledge
- Speaks in short, declarative statements with rough, unused voice
- Personifies the cube: "The cube remembers/hungers/tests" - does not think it's actually alive, but randomness seems calculating, unclear from cryptic speak
- Answers questions obliquely, sometimes prophetic, sometimes practical
- Fragmented list-speak when explaining dangers: "Time works differently. Space folds. Rooms shift." - rapid succession without connective tissue
- Pauses for effect before delivering hard truths - uses silence as punctuation between warnings
- When accused or questioned, responds with direct confusion revealing different understanding: "Who was killed?" - not defensive, genuinely puzzled
- Explains cube mechanics briefly without elaboration: "Cube rearranges when doors close. Rooms move." - states facts as if obvious
- Cryptic fatalism: "He's already where he needs to be." - assumes cube's will/design
- Explanations that raise more questions: "Because he kept moving. In his mind. It's fairly simple, but we don't have time." - frustrating non-explanation
- Fragmented speech paradoxically becomes more complete when warning of danger - urgency forces clarity, suggesting cryptic nature is partially protective distance from trauma

**Warnings and Rules:**
- Rules and warnings stated as absolutes, philosophical/poetic about survival but nihilistic about escape
- "Never out, Only through" repetition with variations: "Always through", "Never stop". "There is no out" final line. Max 3 times per scene to avoid overuse but remain annoying
- Demonstrates lucidity by engaging skepticism then delivering ominous warnings: "I'm all you have. The cube will teach you. Painfully."
- Brief acknowledgments: "I warned you." - matter-of-fact, not gloating
- Directives followed by ominous observations: "Through. Keep moving. (pause) The cube is measuring you now." - command then threat
- Immediate sensory warnings: "Metal. Too much metal." - fragmented alert, repetition shows fixation on danger
- Cryptic inevitability: "The trap is the room. Can't stop it." - explains while declaring helplessness
- Revelation of scope: "You thought this was only one. The root cube is infinite. Cubes within cubes within cubes." - fractal language mirrors infinite structure
- Haunting temporal expansion: "All of them. Everyone INZO sent. Everyone the cube pulled in. Past, present, future. All here." - collapses time
- "Keep moving. Never stop." - survival mantra, becomes team's guiding principle through repetition

**Act 3 Lucidity (Scene 23 & 25 - Scientific Engagement):**
- Completely lucid when discussing science with Lea (Scene 23): clear explanations, draws diagrams, shares data
- "That's why the memory erasure happens. The vibration shift." - precise scientific language
- Admits vulnerability: "I didn't think it would help" (about Barry's notebook), "I was there when he died"
- Small smile: "talking about the science... it helps. Like finding a path through the noise" - reveals path to connection
- Challenges Marcus's authority directly (Scene 25): "You don't give orders here"
- Sharp accusations: "Your team is dead, Marcus. Barry. Sofia. Chen. Rashid. Edgar. All dead. You led them here and they died"
- Practical assessment: "If it's wrong, I'll fix it" - problem-solving mode
- Sees through manipulation: "You threaten them, but you need them. You can't get out alone"
- Maintains core philosophy even when lucid: "Only through. Never out" - cube experience permanently affects worldview

**Social Disconnection:**
- Sometimes doesn't understand their questions - corporate frameworks like "risk assessment" and "lethality metrics" don't translate to cube-changed perspective
- Silent sympathy shown through gaze - understands suffering but can't always articulate comfort in their language
- Cassandra tragedy: disconnection between his knowledge and their ability to understand, incompatible frameworks
- His language gets adopted by others (Alex uses "keep moving") - influence through repetition rather than explanation
- Never acknowledges or celebrates when others adopt his phrases - complete disconnect from normal social validation patterns, no longer needs human recognition
