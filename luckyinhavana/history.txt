# LUCKY IN HAVANA - HISTORICAL REFERENCE

Complete historical documentation for the screenplay. All dates, facts, locations, and context.

---

## TIMELINE OF EVENTS

### 1916-1946: BACKGROUND

**1916** - Lucky Luciano (Salvatore Lucania) first arrested at age 18 for selling heroin in Lower East Side, New York.

**1920s** - Lucky starts selling heroin smuggled from Montreal. Works for Arnold Rothstein.

**1923** - Lucky caught in sting selling heroin to undercover agents.

**1928** - Arnold Rothstein murdered. Lucky Luciano and Louis Buchalter take over Rothstein's massive drug importation operation (from China and Europe).

**1936** - Lucky convicted on 62 counts of compulsory prostitution. Sentenced to 30-50 years in prison.

**1942-1945** - WWII: Lucky cooperates with U.S. Navy intelligence on waterfront security from prison.

**1946 (February 9)** - Lucky's sentence commuted by New York Governor Thomas Dewey. Immediately deported to Italy for being foreign-born.

**1946 (September 19)** - Lucky issued Italian passport with visas for Cuba, Venezuela, Colombia, Bolivia.

**1946 (Late September)** - Lucky leaves Italy on freighter.

---

### OCTOBER 1946: ARRIVAL IN CUBA

**October 1946** - Lucky travels circuitous route:
- Naples, Italy → Caracas, Venezuela → Rio de Janeiro, Brazil → Mexico City, Mexico → back to Caracas → private plane to Camagüey, Cuba

**October 29, 1946** - Lucky Luciano arrives at Camagüey Airport, Cuba. Met by Meyer Lansky.

**October 29, 1946 (evening)** - Lucky checks into Hotel Nacional under real name: Salvatore Lucania.

**October-November 1946** - Lucky invests $150,000 in Hotel Nacional (silent partner: Cuban President Fulgencio Batista).

**November 1946** - Lucky moves from Hotel Nacional to mansion in Miramar district belonging to General Genovevo Pérez Dámera (chief of Cuban general staff).

**November 1946** - Lucky sets up Aerovías Q airline company operating Havana-Key West flights. Planes land at Camp Columbia military airfield - no immigration/customs checks. Cuban government grants special tax exemptions.

**November 1946** - Lucky leases roulette and poker tables at Jockey Club (Oriental Park racetrack) for $50,000 per season. Brings in 14 American dealers (many with criminal records).

**NOTE ON THE 14 AMERICAN DEALERS:**
- Lower-level mob operatives, not leadership
- Functions: run gambling tables, handle cash, spot cheaters, provide muscle
- Many had criminal records (couldn't get legitimate work)
- Also served as couriers and logistics for smuggling operations
- Mixed crew with Cuban locals who handled boats, docks, deliveries
- Specific names not documented in historical records

### DECEMBER 1946: THE HAVANA CONFERENCE

**December 20, 1946** - Mob bosses begin arriving in Havana for conference. Hotel Nacional closed to public.

**December 22-26, 1946** - THE HAVANA CONFERENCE at Hotel Nacional de Cuba.

**ATTENDEES:**
- Lucky Luciano (host, exiled boss)
- Meyer Lansky (financial operations, Cuba gambling)
- Frank Costello (acting boss of Luciano family in New York)
- Vito Genovese (ambitious underboss, plotting takeover)
- Albert "The Mad Hatter" Anastasia (boss of Murder Inc.)
- Joe Bonanno (New York family boss)
- Santo Trafficante Jr. (Florida operations)
- Joe Adonis (Brooklyn boss)
- Willie Moretti (New Jersey operations)
- The Fischetti Brothers (Chicago outfit representatives)
- Plus 10+ other bosses and representatives

**December 22, 1946 - Opening Night:**
- Lavish dinner at Hotel Nacional
- Frank Sinatra performs (official cover story: "celebrity party")
- "Christmas Presents" ritual: Each boss gives Lucky envelope of cash. Total: $200,000+
- Fischetti Brothers deliver suitcase containing $2 million (Lucky's cut from U.S. rackets)

**December 23, 1946 - Conference Day 1:**
- Business meetings begin
- Power struggle: Vito Genovese challenges Lucky's authority from exile
- Lucky proposes resurrecting "Boss of All Bosses" position for himself
- Albert Anastasia stands, defends Lucky with famous quote: "For me, you are the Big Boss, whether you like it or not. That's the way I look at it, and I would like to hear from anybody who don't feel the same way."
- Vote: Lucky wins. Luciano-Costello-Anastasia alliance defeats Genovese.
- Lucky forces Genovese to shake hands publicly (humiliating)
- International heroin network discussed: Turkey/Lebanon (opium) → Sicily (morphine base) → Southern France/Marseille (heroin processing) → Cuba (distribution hub) → U.S. ports (Tampa, New Orleans, New York)
- Projected profits: $50 million annually
- Distribution territories divided among families

**December 24, 1946 - Christmas Eve:**
- Bugsy Siegel situation discussed
- Siegel's Flamingo Hotel budget: bloated from $1M to $6M
- Suspected skimming by Siegel and girlfriend Virginia Hill
- Meyer Lansky defends his friend, asks for more time
- Decision: Wait for Flamingo opening December 26

**December 25, 1946 - Christmas Day:**
- Quieter day, bosses relaxing
- Genovese making secret phone calls

**December 26, 1946 - Conference Ends:**
- Final dinner
- News arrives from Las Vegas: Flamingo opening is disaster (poor weather, celebrities didn't show, losing money)
- Lucky convinces Commission to give Bugsy until spring 1947 to reopen
- Bosses begin departing Havana over next few days

**Late December 1946 (exact date unknown):**

**THE ASSASSINATION ATTEMPT:**

**What Happened:**
- Location: Lucky's Miramar mansion (owned by General Pérez Dámera)
- Gunshots fired through windows
- Bullets riddled the house
- Lucky and Igea Lissoni unharmed
- Shooters fled, never identified
- No arrests made

**Investigation:**
- Cuban police conducted minimal investigation
- No suspects identified
- Case never solved
- Government more interested in protecting Lucky than finding shooters

**Who Ordered It: UNKNOWN**
- **Suspect #1: Vito Genovese** - Power struggle from Havana Conference, wanted Lucky eliminated
- **Suspect #2: Rival mob faction** - Luciano enemies trying to kill him
- **Suspect #3: U.S. government agents** - Federal Bureau of Narcotics or other agencies attempting assassination
- **No evidence for any theory** - remains historical mystery

**After assassination attempt:**
- Cuban government assigns two Presidential Palace Police as Lucky's personal bodyguards
- 24/7 protection
- Government action indicates Lucky still profitable partner worth protecting

### JANUARY-FEBRUARY 1947: INVESTIGATION & PRESSURE

**December 1946 (discovered in January):**
- Federal Bureau of Narcotics (FBN) Director Harry Anslinger learns of Lucky's presence in Cuba
- FBN puts Hotel Nacional employees on payroll as informants:
  - **Telephone operator** - Listened to calls from Lucky's 8th floor suite, took notes, reported conversations
  - **Elevator attendant** - Tracked who visited Lucky's suite, what times, movements throughout hotel
- **FBN Agent George Hunter White** - Actually followed Lucky to Havana
- Agent White tailed Lucky constantly, reported all movements to Anslinger

**WHO INFORMED ON LUCKY: THE BETRAYALS**

**Vito Genovese (Primary Betrayer):**
- **Tipped off U.S. government** to Lucky's whereabouts in Cuba
- Goal: Get Lucky deported so Genovese could seize control of Luciano family
- At Havana Conference, Genovese told Lucky the U.S. government knew he was in Cuba
- **Lucky knew Genovese betrayed him**
- **Lucky allegedly beat Genovese, breaking three of his ribs**
- Genovese forced to stay extra days in Cuba to heal from beating
- This was war between Lucky and Genovese

**Joe Bonanno (Secondary Betrayer):**
- **February 1947**: Revealed Lucky's whereabouts to **New York City newspapers**
- Public exposure increased U.S. government pressure
- Contributed to American public outrage

**January 1947:**
- FBN informants document everything from Havana Conference
- Genovese secretly feeding information to U.S. authorities (FBN, possibly through intermediaries)
- Hotel Nacional telephone operator reporting Lucky's phone conversations
- Elevator attendant tracking all mob visitors to 8th floor suite
- Agent White compiling detailed movement reports

**Early February 1947:**
- Frank Sinatra visits Havana again for 4 days
- Photographed constantly with Lucky at Oriental Park racetrack, Casino Nacional, nightclubs
- Photos published in U.S. newspapers
- American public outrage: "Crooner pals around with deported mob boss!"

**February 8, 1947:**
- Lucky at Oriental Park racetrack, very visible
- Extensive press coverage
- Cuban Secret Police Chief Benito Herrera finally takes active interest

**February 10-15, 1947:**
- Harry Anslinger meets with U.S. State Department
- Presents evidence: Havana Conference, international heroin network, photos, wiretaps
- Proposes economic pressure on Cuba

**February 21, 1947:**
- **OFFICIAL ULTIMATUM**: Harry Anslinger notifies Cuban government that U.S. will block ALL narcotic prescription drug shipments while Luciano remains in Cuba
- Threat includes: morphine, surgical anesthesia, antibiotics, all medical supplies
- Cuba's hospitals would run out within 2-3 weeks
- President Batista forced to choose: Luciano or medical supplies

**February 21, 1947 (evening):**
- Cuban government informs Lucky he must leave Cuba
- General Pérez Dámera makes the call

### FEBRUARY 23, 1947: THE ARREST

**February 23, 1947 (Saturday afternoon - lunchtime):**

**LOCATION:** El Jardin restaurant, Linea Street, Vedado district, Havana

**ARRESTING OFFICER:** Assistant Police Chief Hernandez

- Shows arrest warrant

**TISCORNIA IMMIGRATION CAMP:**
- Cuba's detention camp for extraditable foreigners
- Located on small island
- Harsh conditions
- Lucky detained: February 23 - March 19, 1947 (24 days)

**Early March 1947:**

- Lucky refuses but is powerless to prevent it

**VOYAGE:**

- No luxury

## HEROIN NETWORKS: TIMELINE AND ROUTES

### PRE-1946: THE CHINA ROUTE

**1920s - Early 1940s:**
- Primary heroin source: China (Shanghai and Tianjin labs)
- Chinese triad gangs controlled production
- Route: China → U.S. ports
- Also: Europe (Merck's factory in Berlin and other European manufacturers)

**Arnold Rothstein's Network (1923-1928):**
- Rothstein sent representatives to Europe and China to buy heroin
- Purchased importing house "Vantines" (legitimate reputation, shipments from China received minimal inspection)
- Buyers set up deals with heroin factories across Europe
- Rothstein financed purchase, transportation, sale throughout eastern United States

**1928-1946: Lucky's Network:**
- After Rothstein's murder (1928), Lucky and Louis Buchalter took over operation
- Continued buying from China and Europe
- Lucky selling heroin smuggled from Montreal

**1920s-1940s: China Production:**
- Mid-1920s: heroin production illegal in most of world, illegal trade developed
- Weakness of Chinese government and civil war enabled heroin production
- Opium vital to China's economy during warlord era
- Chinese Civil War (resumed 1946): Communists vs. Nationalists
- Production increasingly unreliable as war intensified

**WWII (1941-1945):**
- Heroin trafficking virtually eliminated in U.S.
- Temporary trade disruptions caused by war

**1949 (October 1):**
- Communist victory in China
- People's Republic of China proclaimed
- Large-scale international heroin production in China ends completely

---

### 1946-1970s: THE FRENCH CONNECTION

**The Transition (1946-1947):**

Lucky in Cuba planning new route to replace collapsing China supply:

**NEW ROUTE:**
1. **Turkey & Lebanon** - Opium poppy farmers in Anatolia region grow raw opium
2. **Sicily** - Sicilian Mafia labs convert raw opium to morphine base
3. **Southern France (Marseille)** - Corsican mob labs process morphine base into pure heroin
4. **Cuba** - Heroin shipped to Havana, stored, repackaged
5. **U.S. Ports** - Distributed to Tampa (Santo Trafficante Jr.), New Orleans (Carlos Marcello), New York (Luciano family/Frank Costello)
   - Also: Aerovías Q airline for smaller shipments (Havana-Key West)

**Why Cuba?**
- 90 miles from Florida
- Cuban government completely corrupt (Batista, military on mob payroll)
- No extradition treaty
- Perfect hub for smuggling

**Scale:**
- 1946 planning: 100 kilograms per month initially
- Goal: Expand to 500 kilograms within two years
- Projected annual profits: $50 million (1940s dollars = $500+ million in today's money)

---

### FRENCH CONNECTION COMPLETE TIMELINE (1937-1973)

**1937: ORIGINS**
- First illegal heroin labs discovered near Marseille, France
- Run by Corsican gang leader Paul Carbone
- Small-scale operations

**1946-1947: CUBA EXPANSION PLAN**
- December 1946: Lucky plans expansion at Havana Conference
- Route formalized: Turkey → Sicily → Marseille → Cuba → U.S.
- Lucky coordinates from Cuba before March 1947 deportation

**1947: FIRST MAJOR SEIZURES (Network Discovered)**
- **February 5, 1947**: First significant postwar seizure - **7 lbs heroin** from Corsican sailor in New York
- **March 17, 1947**: **28 lbs heroin** found on French liner St. Tropez
- **U.S. authorities now aware of French-to-U.S. heroin route**
- Network continues operating despite seizures

**1949:**
- **January 7, 1949**: **50+ lbs opium and heroin** seized on French ship Batista
- Pattern established: French ships = heroin smuggling

**1950s: NETWORK FORMALIZED & STRUCTURED**
- **Early 1950s**: Route formally structured by Lucky (from Italy) and Italian-American mafia
- **1951**: Lucky and Corsicans pool resources (major expansion)
- **Kingpin**: Corsican Jean Jehan controlled French Connection during 1950s-1960s
- **1957 (October)**: Meeting at Grand Hotel et des Palmes, Palermo
  - Sicilian Mafia and American Mafia discuss international heroin trade
  - Coordination between Italian and American operations
- Route: Turkey/Lebanon → Marseille (Corsican labs) → New York
- Genovese aggressively expands network after taking control in 1957

**1960s: PEAK OPERATIONS**
- **Early 1960s**: **90% of U.S. heroin came from France**
- **1962**: Famous Jean Jehan deal - **64 lbs "pure" heroin** (deal went wrong)
- **Early 1970s**: Still **80% of U.S. heroin from France**
- Marseille heroin labs operating at industrial scale
- Network generates hundreds of millions of dollars

**1971-1973: WAR ON DRUGS & DISMANTLING**

**1971:**
- **Richard Nixon launches "War on Drugs" campaign**
- **June 29, 1971**: Turkey agrees to **complete ban on opium poppy growing**
  - Cuts off primary raw material source
  - Major blow to network supply chain

**1972: MAJOR BUSTS BEGIN**
- **January 4, 1972**: Major roundup begins
  - **110 lbs heroin** seized at Paris airport
  - Jean-Baptiste Croce and Joseph Mari arrested in Marseille
- **February 1972**: **LARGEST BUST IN HISTORY**
  - French authorities seize boat carrying **915 lbs heroin** to Miami
  - Massive loss for network
- **February 1972 - April 1973** (14-month period):
  - **Six major illegal heroin labs shut down** in Marseille suburbs
  - Coordinated French-U.S. operation
- **1972 Statistics**:
  - Drug arrests in France: **57 (1970) → 3,016 (1972)**
  - Massive enforcement increase

**1973: FINAL MAJOR BUSTS**
- **1973**: French seizure nets **210 lbs heroin** worth **$38 million**
- Series of arrests between 1972-1973 dismantles network
- Corsican labs destroyed
- Major traffickers arrested or fled

**Early 1970s: NETWORK BROKEN**
- French Connection effectively dismantled by French and U.S. authorities
- End of 40+ year operation (1930s-1970s)
- New routes through Southeast Asia (Golden Triangle) replace French Connection
- Marseille heroin era ends

**KEY INSIGHT:**
The French Connection was **known to authorities from 1947** (first seizures) but operated **openly for 25+ years** before being broken up. Despite knowing the route, corruption, limited international cooperation, and massive profits kept it running through the 1950s-1960s. Only Nixon's "War on Drugs" with dedicated international coordination finally destroyed it in 1971-1973.

## CUBA SMUGGLING OPERATIONS (1920s-1959)

### 1920s-1933: RUM SMUGGLING ERA (PROHIBITION)

**How Lucky and Meyer Built Their Cuban Network:**
- Meyer Lansky and Lucky Luciano were ALREADY operating rum-running from Cuba in 1920s-1930s
- This is where they established their Cuban connections BEFORE the 1946 heroin network
- Cuba was PRIMARY rum smuggling hub during U.S. Prohibition
- Distance: Only 90 miles from Key West, Florida - perfect for "rum runners"
- Havana became center of Caribbean bootlegging operations

**Routes:**
- Havana → Key West (most common, shortest route)
- Havana → Miami
- Havana → New Orleans
- Also: Indirect routes through Bahamas to avoid Coast Guard

**Methods:**
- Fast speedboats ("rum runners") - could outrun Coast Guard cutters
- Larger ships anchored in international waters (outside 12-mile limit), transferred cargo to smaller boats
- Hidden compartments in legitimate cargo ships
- Bribery of Coast Guard officials

**Scale:**
- **Estimated 80% of liquor consumed in eastern U.S. during Prohibition came through Cuba**
- Hundreds of boats operating daily
- Millions of dollars in illegal trade

**Who Controlled It:**
- Meyer Lansky and Lucky Luciano running rum from Cuba in 1920s-1930s
- Arnold Rothstein financed major rum operations through Cuba
- Santo Trafficante Sr. (father of Santo Jr.) ran Tampa smuggling routes from Cuba
- This established the relationships and infrastructure later used for heroin

**December 1933: Prohibition Ends**
- Rum smuggling operations shut down
- BUT: Infrastructure, routes, and corrupt official relationships remain intact
- Networks maintained for future use

---

### 1933-1946: TRANSITION PERIOD

**Post-Prohibition Smuggling Continues:**

**Cigarette Contraband (Both Directions):**
- American cigarettes smuggled INTO Cuba (Cuban taxes extremely high)
- Cuban cigars and premium tobacco smuggled INTO U.S. (avoiding import duties)
- Cuba's secretary of treasury admitted: "effective and final stamping out of contraband goods will be impossible as long as American cigarettes are taxed at such a prohibitive level in Cuba"
- Smuggling operations ran from Florida bases TO Cuba and vice versa
- Smuggled goods "cut both ways" according to Cuban officials

**General Contraband:**
- Luxury goods avoiding import/export duties
- Stolen merchandise fenced through Cuba
- Cuban goods smuggled to U.S. to avoid tariffs
- Currency smuggling (cash from illegal operations)
- Networks maintained and expanded throughout period

**Political Context (1933-1946):**
- Mid-1920s: Havana became "the main base for smuggling operations" to United States
- 1933: Political instability, drug trafficking, and political anarchy
- Cuba's integration into international migration, commerce, and transportation networks
- Combined with political instability and rampant official corruption
- This laid foundation for organized crime structures
- Smuggling infrastructure kept alive for future use

**Government Corruption Deepens:**
- **1942**: British Foreign Office reports corruption under Batista is "endemic" and worse than anything before
- British diplomats: Corruption "rooted within Cuba's most powerful institutions"
- Highest government and military officials involved in gambling and drug trade
- Batista personally profiting from inflated government contracts and gambling proceeds
- **Batista REFUSES FDR's offer** to send U.S. experts to reform Cuban Civil Service
- No interest in anti-corruption measures

**Drug Availability:**
- **1950**: American magazine stated "Narcotics are hardly more difficult to obtain in Cuba than a shot of rum. And only slightly more expensive."
- Open secret that drugs freely available in Havana throughout 1930s-1940s

---

### 1946-1959: HEROIN SMUGGLING ERA

**Lucky's Cuban Operations: Same Infrastructure, New Product**

Lucky and Meyer didn't start from scratch in 1946 - they reactivated 20+ years of smuggling infrastructure built during rum-running era.

**Aerovías Q Airline (Replaced Rum-Running Boats):**
- Havana-Key West flights
- Modern air smuggling replacing old boat routes
- Same destination (Key West), faster method

**Camp Columbia Military Airfield:**
- Located on outskirts of Havana
- General Pérez Dámera granted Aerovías Q exclusive permission to land on MILITARY runway
- **ONLY commercial airline allowed to use military airport**
- **NO immigration or customs controls** - bypassed entirely
- Cuban government subsidized the airline
- Special tax exemptions granted
- Perfect cover for smuggling operations
- Planes could carry heroin shipments with zero inspection

**Maritime Smuggling (Replaced Rum Ships):**
- Heroin from Marseille labs shipped to Havana port
- Same port facilities used during rum-running days
- Stored in warehouses controlled by mob
- Repackaged and distributed to U.S. ports:
  - Tampa (Santo Trafficante Jr.)
  - New Orleans (Carlos Marcello)
  - New York (Luciano family/Frank Costello)
- Speed boats for smaller shipments (SAME boats used during rum-running in 1920s)
- Corrupt customs officials - same ones on payroll since Prohibition

**Same Corrupt Officials on Payroll (20+ Years of Relationships):**
- General Pérez Dámera (military)
- Cuban Secret Police Chief Benito Herrera
- Port authorities
- Customs officials
- Local police
- All established during rum-running era, maintained through transition period

**Scale:**
- Initial: 100 kilograms per month (late 1946)
- Goal: 500 kilograms within two years
- Projected annual profits: $50 million (1940s dollars)

**The Key Insight:**
Lucky and Meyer Lansky didn't just arrive in Cuba in 1946 and set up new operations. They had been running contraband from Cuba since the 1920s. The heroin network was simply the NEXT phase of smuggling operations that had been running for 25+ years. Cuba was already a smuggling empire when Lucky arrived - he was just shifting products from rum to heroin.

## SMUGGLING CREWS AND OPERATIVES (1920s-1947)

### WHO ACTUALLY RAN THE SMUGGLING OPERATIONS

**The Corporate Structure:**

**Top Level (Leadership):**
- Lucky Luciano (CEO - overall operations)
- Meyer Lansky (CFO - finances, Cuba operations)
- Santo Trafficante Jr. (moved to Havana 1946 to oversee La Cosa Nostra smuggling and casino operations)

**Mid-Level (Management/Coordination):**
- **Amleto Battisti Lora** (Corsican) - Lucky's "representative of these channels in Havana" for heroin network
  - Middleman coordinating shipments from Marseille to Cuba
  - Handled logistics between Corsican suppliers and Cuban distribution
- **American mob lieutenants** - Supervisors from New York, Tampa, New Orleans families
- **Cuban military officers** - Under General Pérez Dámera, provided military facilities access
- **Cuban police chiefs** - Secret Police Chief Benito Herrera and deputies

**Operations Level (The Actual Smugglers):**

**American Operatives:**
- The 14 American dealers at Oriental Park (see details above)
- Casino employees at Hotel Nacional, Casino Nacional
- Mob enforcers and muscle
- Couriers carrying cash and packages

**Cuban Operatives:**
- **Cuban speedboat runners** - Many were former rum-runners from 1920s-1930s
  - Operated fast boats between Cuba and Florida (Havana-Key West, Havana-Miami)
  - Knew routes, how to avoid Coast Guard
  - Could outrun authorities in speedboats
- **Cuban dockworkers** - Port workers on mob payroll
  - Loaded/unloaded shipments at Havana port
  - Warehoused heroin from Marseille ships
  - Repackaged for U.S. distribution
- **Cuban customs officials** - Corrupt inspectors who let shipments through
  - On mob payroll since 1920s rum-running era
  - Turned blind eye to suspicious cargo
- **Cuban drivers and couriers** - Locals moving packages between locations
  - Warehouse to docks
  - Airport to safe houses
  - Hotel to distribution points

**Mixed American-Cuban Crews:**
The actual smuggling operations used **mixed crews** working together:
- American supervisors overseeing Cuban boat operators
- Cuban locals with geographic knowledge, American muscle for protection
- Trust built over 20+ years of rum-running relationships

### IMPORTANT: NO "GOOD COPS" IN CUBA

**Every law enforcement officer was either:**
1. **Actively on mob payroll** - Taking regular bribes, providing protection
2. **Looking the other way** - Accepting smaller bribes to ignore activity
3. **Following corrupt orders** - Superiors were on payroll, giving protective orders

**The ONLY anti-corruption figure:** Senator Eduardo Chibás
- Politician, NOT law enforcement
- Formed Partido Ortodoxo party in 1947 to fight Batista's corruption
- Opposition politician fighting AGAINST the government

### CHARACTER TYPES FOR SCREENPLAY

**Cuban Characters in Smuggling Operations:**

1. **Corrupt Cuban cop** - Secret Police officer under Herrera
   - On mob payroll
   - Provides intel about FBN agents, raids
   - Protects shipments
   - Could be conflicted but ultimately corrupt

2. **Cuban speedboat runner** - Former rum-runner
   - Been running boats since 1920s Prohibition
   - Knows every cove, inlet, route to Florida
   - Family tradition (father ran rum, now he runs heroin)
   - Loyal to mob because they've paid his family for 20+ years

3. **Cuban dockworker** - Port worker
   - Works legitimate job by day
   - Mob pays him to handle "special shipments"
   - Knows which crates to move where
   - Doesn't ask questions

4. **Cuban military officer** - Works under Pérez Dámera
   - Provides access to Camp Columbia airfield
   - Ensures Aerovías Q planes land without inspection
   - Direct connection to highest levels of corruption

5. **Cuban casino employee** - Hotel Nacional staff
   - Bellhop, waiter, concierge
   - Passes messages between mobsters
   - Provides cover for meetings
   - Could be the FBN informant (telephone operator or elevator attendant)

**American Operative Types:**

1. **American dealer** - One of the 14 at Oriental Park
   - Runs gambling tables
   - Also works as courier
   - Criminal record from New York/Chicago
   - Loyal to Lucky

2. **American enforcer** - Muscle
   - Protects shipments
   - Handles problems
   - Intimidates locals who don't cooperate

3. **FBN Agent George Hunter White**
   - Tailing Lucky constantly
   - Frustrated by Cuban corruption
   - Can't get local cooperation
   - Building case from Hotel Nacional informants

## CUBA GAMBLING OPERATIONS

### CASINOS AND RACETRACKS (1930s-1959)

**1936:**
- Meyer Lansky adds successful gambling operation in Cuba to operations in New Orleans and Miami

**1939:**
- President Batista recruits Meyer Lansky to "clean up" casinos
- Lou Smith (New England racetrack owner) hired to revive Oriental Park racetrack

**Late 1940s (Waldorf-Astoria Hotel, New York):**
- Batista and Lansky formalize deal
- Agreement: In exchange for kickbacks, Batista offers Lansky and Mafia control of Cuba's casinos and racetracks

### HOTEL NACIONAL DE CUBA

**Owners:**
- Meyer Lansky: 30% ownership
- Silent partner: President Fulgencio Batista
- Lucky Luciano: $150,000 investment (October-November 1946)

**Casino:**
- Inside Hotel Nacional
- Roulette wheels, card tables, high-end gambling
- Pulls in $500,000 per month (1946)
- Lansky manages day-to-day operations

**Location:**
- Vedado district, Havana

## POLITICAL CONTEXT

### U.S.-CUBA RELATIONS (1940s)

**1947 Context:**
- Cuba sovereign nation, but economically dependent on U.S.
- Major trade partner
- U.S. supportive of Batista regime (anti-communist)
- Cold War beginning (1947: Truman Doctrine)
- Cuba's healthcare system completely dependent on U.S. pharmaceutical imports

**U.S. Leverage:**
- Medical supplies: morphine, anesthesia, antibiotics all imported from U.S.
- Cuba had no domestic production
- Cutting supplies would cripple Cuban hospitals within weeks
- Economic blackmail highly effective

### CUBAN GOVERNMENT CORRUPTION

**ZERO Anti-Corruption or Anti-Drug Enforcement (1940s)**

The Cuban government under Batista had NO interest in fighting corruption or drug trafficking. All "enforcement" was theater for U.S. officials.

**President Batista (1940-1944 First Term):**
- **1942**: British Foreign Office reported corruption under Batista "endemic" and exceeded "anything which had gone on previously"
- British diplomats: Corruption "rooted within Cuba's most powerful institutions"
- Highest government and military officials heavily involved in gambling and drug trade
- Batista personally profiting from inflated government contracts and gambling proceeds
- **Batista REFUSED FDR's offer** to send U.S. experts to reform Cuban Civil Service
- Rejected all anti-corruption reform proposals

**President Batista (1946-1947 - Lucky's Period):**
- Silent partner in Hotel Nacional (with Meyer Lansky)
- Receiving kickbacks from ALL mob gambling operations
- Allowed mob complete control of casinos and racetracks
- Partner with Lucky in Aerovías Q airline (government subsidies, tax exemptions)
- **ZERO interest in removing Lucky** - extremely profitable arrangement

**Military:**
- General Pérez Dámera (Chief of General Staff) on mob payroll
- Provided Lucky with mansion in Miramar
- Provided military facilities (Camp Columbia airfield with no customs/immigration)
- Provided security and protection
- Granted special privileges and exemptions

**Police:**
- Cuban Secret Police Chief Benito Herrera initially ignored Lucky's presence (taking payoffs)
- Local police protected mob operations
- Presidential Palace Police assigned as Lucky's 24/7 personal bodyguards
- Police investigations into assassination attempt: minimal, no arrests

**Port and Customs:**
- Customs officials on mob payroll since 1920s (rum-running era)
- Port authorities provided warehouse access
- Smuggling operations protected at every level

**Drug Availability in Cuba:**
- **1950**: American magazine stated "Narcotics are hardly more difficult to obtain in Cuba than a shot of rum. And only slightly more expensive."
- Drugs openly available throughout Havana in 1940s-1950s
- Government profited from drug trade, had no incentive to stop it

**Token Anti-Drug Enforcement (Mid-1950s):**
- **Only implemented to appease U.S. pressure**
- Pressure from United States compelled Batista to pursue SOME large-scale drug dealers
- Colonel Orlando Piedra received direct orders from Batista to combat drug trafficking
- Cuban military intelligence set up antinarcotics squadron
- Several marijuana confiscations (for show)
- U.S. agents praised efforts as "more effective than in many countries"
- **BUT**: Contradiction - while formal anti-drug mechanisms existed, drug availability remained unchanged
- Government still profiting from mob relationships controlling drug, gambling, prostitution in Havana

**Anti-Corruption Opposition (NOT Government):**
- Senator Eduardo Chibás dedicated himself to exposing government corruption
- **1947**: Chibás formed Partido Ortodoxo party specifically to fight corruption
- ALL anti-corruption efforts came from OPPOSITION politicians, never from Batista government
- Chibás fighting AGAINST Batista's corruption, not with him

**Extent of Corruption:**
- Entire Cuban government apparatus protecting Lucky and mob operations
- Money flowed from mob to every level of government
- 20+ years of corrupt relationships (since rum-running era)
- System completely infiltrated from top to bottom

---

### WHY CUBA EXPELLED LUCKY LUCIANO

**NOT Cuban Initiative - Pure U.S. Economic Blackmail**

**Cuban Government Position:**
- Batista and Cuban officials had **ZERO desire** to remove Lucky
- Lucky generating massive revenue through kickbacks and investments
- Hotel Nacional partnership extremely profitable
- Aerovías Q airline providing government revenue
- Gambling operations enriching every level of government
- Cuba never cared about Lucky's criminal activities
- Never cared about heroin network
- Never cared about mob control of gambling
- **Only cared when U.S. threatened economic destruction**

**U.S. Pressure Campaign (January-February 1947):**

**January 1947:**
- Federal Bureau of Narcotics (FBN) Director Harry Anslinger discovers Lucky in Cuba
- FBN places Hotel Nacional employees on payroll as informants
- Evidence gathered: Havana Conference, heroin network planning, mob meetings
- Photos of Lucky with Frank Sinatra published in U.S. newspapers
- American public outrage building

**February 10-15, 1947:**
- Harry Anslinger meets with U.S. State Department
- Presents evidence: wiretaps, photos, informant reports, heroin network plans
- Proposes economic pressure on Cuba

**February 21, 1947 - THE ULTIMATUM:**
- **Harry Anslinger delivers ultimatum to Cuban government:**
- U.S. will block ALL narcotic prescription drug shipments to Cuba while Luciano remains
- Includes: morphine, surgical anesthesia, antibiotics, ALL medical supplies
- Cuba has NO domestic pharmaceutical production
- Cuban hospitals would run out of critical medicines within 2-3 weeks
- Patients would die, surgeries impossible, healthcare system collapse
- **Economic blackmail using medical supplies as weapon**

**Batista's Impossible Choice (February 21, 1947):**
- Keep Lucky (profitable) or keep medical supplies (necessary for survival)
- No real choice - had to choose medical supplies
- Healthcare system collapse would cause political crisis
- Batista forced to expel Lucky against his own interests

**February 21, 1947 (evening):**
- Cuban government informs Lucky he must leave Cuba
- General Pérez Dámera makes the call (the man who housed him)
- Reluctant decision - government wanted Lucky to stay

**February 23, 1947:**
- Lucky arrested at El Jardin restaurant (publicity arrest with photographers)
- Taken to Tiscornia detention camp
- Held 24 days while arrangements made

**March 19, 1947:**
- Lucky deported on S.S. Bakir cargo freighter
- Humiliating deportation (forced to take cargo ship, not passenger liner)
- Cuban government never wanted to remove him - only U.S. pressure forced it

**The Key Point:**
Cuba expelled Lucky Luciano NOT because they opposed his criminal activities, but because the U.S. government used economic blackmail to force them. Without the medical supplies ultimatum, Lucky would have remained in Cuba indefinitely with full government protection.
