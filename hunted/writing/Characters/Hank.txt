# HANK REEVES

## BASIC INFORMATION
- **Full Name**: Hank Reeves
- **Age**: Early 40s
- **Occupation**: Construction foreman (explains practical building skills)
- **Family**: Married to Sarah, father of Eli (18), Emma (12), Olivia (10)
- **Hunting**: Has his own hunting award - taught whole family to hunt

## ARCHETYPE
**The Protector / The Mentor** - Family's center, teaches self-sufficiency and survival. His competence makes him both admirable and a target. His death drives the entire revenge plot.

## CHARACTER FUNCTION
The catalyst. His murder drives the entire second and third acts. His prepper skills and teachings become the foundation for both his family's defense and his legacy.

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION
- Build like a thinner, younger John Goodman - big frame but not overweight
- Booming voice that carries across rooms
- Presence that fills a space without trying

## PERSONALITY TRAITS
- Prepper and homesteader at heart
- Construction/architecture background - knows how buildings work
- Knowledgeable, confident, capable
- Teaches family self-sufficiency (gardening, canning, carpentry, home defense)
- Taught entire family to hunt - all have their own awards
- Not paranoid, just prepared
- Formidable opponent who nearly wins against Liam

## RELATIONSHIPS
- Sarah: wife, partner in prepper lifestyle
- Children: teacher, protector
- Bobby: longtime friend, hunting buddy - can be himself around him
- Liam: unknowing target, fights back with everything

## CHARACTER ARC
- **Beginning State (Act 1)**: Content family man, prepper by philosophy not paranoia. Confident in his skills, proud of his family. Living his best life.
- **Inciting Incident**: Unknowingly marks himself as Liam's target through his displayed competence at Cabela's.
- **Escalation (Act 2)**: Recognizes the threat immediately. Fights with everything he has—nearly wins multiple times despite being ambushed and wounded.
- **Crisis Point**: Stabbed, dying, but his final words protect his family even in death: "My family. They're not part of this."
- **End State**: Dies, but his legacy lives on. His teachings enable his family to fight back. His spirit haunts the final act through the skills he passed down. A Flat Arc: his beliefs were right, but the world still took him.

## DIALOGUE STYLE
- Big personality - says likeable things without trying
- Booming voice matches his presence
- Natural storyteller - draws people in when he talks (Olivia's fish story)
- Comfortable teasing friends, warm with family
- Explains things with enthusiasm, not condescension - genuinely wants to share knowledge
- Uses folksy wisdom but credits sources ("JFK said it, asshole")
- Short, meaningful statements for important points ("It's not easy. It's just worth it.")
- Comfortable admitting others are better than him ("She'd gut you before you knew she was there")
- Secure masculinity - can be "whipped" and happy about it

## KEY SCENES
- **Act 1, Scene 3: The Cabela's Encounter** - First appearance. Demonstrates expertise at Cabela's with friend Bobby—knowledgeable about emergency rations, prefers knives over guns close-quarters: "Knife doesn't jam. Doesn't run out of ammo. Doesn't make noise." Unknowingly marks himself as Liam's target.
- **Act 1, Scene 4: Family Time** - Teaches self-defense "homeschool" to the kids. Patient instructor, uses Bobby as a comedic example of "what not to do." Center of the family—everyone looks to him. "Homeschool first. Then we eat."
- **Act 2, Scene 7: The Flat Tire** - Recognizes sabotage instantly. Dodges arrows, uses flashbang. Hand impaled on nail trap, loses gun. Headbutts Liam, kicks him off porch. Gets inside despite severe hand wound. Proves he's formidable.
- **Act 2, Scene 8: The Battle Inside** - Final stand. Uses blackbelt tourniquet and sai. Nearly wins multiple times—disarms Liam, dominates with skill—but wounded hand and Liam's dirty fighting overcome him. Stabbed with table leg. Last words: "My family. They're not part of this." Dies protecting them even in death.
